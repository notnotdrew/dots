# Product Brief for Kabletown Support Assistant

Our automated support agent, Helpy McHelpface, will revolutionize technical and account support at Kabletown.

## Product Thesis

We make two basic claims:

**Reduced user frustration**
Users are frustrated with our current assistant, which performs poorly on most requests and does not understand when to delegate to a human agent, getting this wrong 80% of the time. Helpy McHelpface will handle direct communication better because it understands user intent more reliably, lowering frustration.

**Lowered support toil**
Today's assistant must bring in a human whenever it wants to make a change. By giving Helpy tools to update accounts, create appointments, or order shipped packages, Kabletown can automate more than half of the remaining unautomated support volume. Faster handling of routine but tedious actions improves wait times and user happiness while easing the backlog for support representatives.

## Antithesis/Risks

What might cause this to not work as we expect?

- Helpy will take actions it should not, causing users to be unpleasantly surprised.
- This may require an autonomous planning agent rather than a simpler assistant, increasing cost and complexity.
- The knowledge base may not provide the context Helpy needs.

## Target Audience

We have two target personas, though we may start with one or the other:

**Account support user**
Tinker Tia is a longtime Kabletown internet and cable TV customer who wants to make changes to her account, such as upgrading or downgrading service or adding hardware.

**Technical support user**
Sad Lisa is a newly joined Kabletown internet customer who has something broken and wants it fixed quickly.

## Product Goals

The primary goals are to increase user happiness and improve the support backlog without hiring substantially more people. The short-term goal is not profitability expansion.

**Adoption metric**
Increase the number of fully automated support interactions from 15% to 65%.

**Value metric**
Improve customer satisfaction ratings after assistant and human support interactions from 2.1 to 3.5 out of 5.

**KPI**
Profit-neutral. Kabletown may reduce human support expense over time, but it may also lower revenue by making downgrades easier. Track downgrades to understand revenue impact.

## North Star Scenarios

**Cable box installation**
Tinker Tia wants a new cable box for a second TV in her bedroom. She goes to `kabletown.com` on her laptop and finds Helpy. He agrees to help and asks whether she will require installer assistance, and she says yes. It guides her through the plan change, gets agreement, and creates a shipping order. It pings the chat when the order ships, then works with her to schedule installation after shipment arrival. Once the technician reports the install is complete, Helpy checks back with her and sends a survey.

**Downgrade**
Tinker Tia has two cable boxes but never uses the one in her bedroom and wants to reduce her monthly bill. Helpy searches for a plan like hers without the extra box and proposes it along with the savings. She accepts. Helpy tells her the reduction will be processed once she returns the box, then sends packaging for the return. After the device is received, Helpy sends her a survey.

**Angry customer with account change**
Tinker Tia wants to reduce her monthly bill by removing an unused cable box. She checks her account and finds a link to Helpy. Helpy notices she is on a promotional rate and tells her there is no available plan change that will save money. She becomes upset and demands a supervisor. A support representative inherits the chat from Helpy and takes over. The representative arranges a small one-time discount in exchange for returning the box. She approves and later receives a survey.

**Outage**
Sad Lisa's cable internet stops working. She opens the Kabletown app on her phone over cellular, sees the Helpy button, and asks what is going on. Helpy checks her address against the outage map, discovers an outage, and sends an ETA. When service is restored, it follows up and sends a satisfaction survey.

**Customer hardware problem**
Sad Lisa's internet is slow, and she blames Kabletown. She finds the Helpy support entry point on `kabletown.com` and starts a conversation. Helpy asks questions about her setup and discovers she is on a Windows laptop over Wi-Fi. It determines there is no outage, then guides her through diagnostics and suggestions, starting with interventions that historically work most often. Resetting the Kabletown-supplied wireless router solves the problem. Helpy tells her to return if the issue keeps happening, then sends a survey and she gives strong ratings.

## Why This Example Works

### Thesis Claims

- They are specific.
- They are falsifiable.
- They explain the value mechanism rather than just naming a feature.

### Risks

- Each risk could invalidate a thesis claim.
- The risks cover technical feasibility, context quality, and user trust.

### Personas

- The names are memorable.
- The personas represent distinct support jobs.
- Each has a clear goal.

### Metrics

- Adoption has a baseline and target.
- Value is measured as an outcome.
- The KPI is honest about downside risk.

### Scenarios

- They cover happy path, alternative path, and escalation.
- They map back to the thesis.
- They end with value capture rather than simple task completion.
