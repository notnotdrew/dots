# Shape Up Canon

Core shaping concepts to preserve when writing pitches.

## Appetite

Appetite is the fixed time budget for the bet.

- `Small batch`: about 2 weeks
- `Big batch`: about 6 weeks

Treat appetite as a constraint, not an estimate. If the work does not fit, cut scope or change the appetite. Do not hedge.

## Shaped Solution

A shaped solution defines the domain the team should work inside without dictating the final implementation.

Good shaping names:

- the area of the product involved
- the main elements or affordances
- the boundary around the work

Bad shaping drifts into:

- pixel-perfect UI
- implementation task lists
- vague wishes with no domain boundary

## Rabbit Holes

Rabbit holes are unknowns or complexity traps that could blow the appetite.

For each one:

1. Name the unknown
2. Say why it is risky
3. Say how it is handled now

Possible resolutions:

- decide now
- rule it out
- timebox a spike

## No-gos

No-gos are explicit out-of-scope statements that protect the team during execution.

Write them flatly:

- Not multi-org support
- Not a new admin UI
- Not a Slack integration in this bet

## Fat-Marker Fidelity

Describe the solution roughly enough to leave room for design during the build.

Useful forms:

- short structural prose
- breadboard-style bullets
- rough sketches or ASCII layouts

Avoid polished mockups or detailed component decisions.
