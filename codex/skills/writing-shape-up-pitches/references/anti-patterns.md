# Pitch Anti-Patterns

Catch these before returning the draft.

## Appetite as estimate

**Smell**: "About 3-4 weeks."

**Why it matters**: The constraint disappears and scope pressure moves into the build.

**Instead**: Pick a fixed appetite and cut scope if needed.

## Spec masquerading as pitch

**Smell**: Detailed screens, implementation choices, or polished UI.

**Why it matters**: The team inherits a spec instead of a shaped bet.

**Instead**: Use fat-marker descriptions of elements and boundaries.

## Wish-list pitch

**Smell**: "We need a better way to X" with no concrete solution shape.

**Why it matters**: The team has to reshape the work during the cycle.

**Instead**: Name the product area, the main elements, and the boundary.

## Hidden rabbit holes

**Smell**: No unknowns named, or vague promises to "figure it out later."

**Why it matters**: The risk detonates during the bet instead of during shaping.

**Instead**: Name the rabbit hole and how it is addressed.

## Empty no-gos

**Smell**: No explicit out-of-scope items.

**Why it matters**: Adjacent asks become scope creep during execution.

**Instead**: State flat boundaries the team can defend later.

## Generic problem framing

**Smell**: "Users want a better experience."

**Why it matters**: Generic problems produce generic solutions.

**Instead**: Name the person, moment, and failure case.
