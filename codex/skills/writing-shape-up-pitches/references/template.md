# Shape Up Pitch Template

Use this when returning the final pitch. Keep it rough enough to leave room for design during execution.

```markdown
# <Pitch Title>

## Pitch Summary

<1-2 sentences describing the bet and why it matters now.>

## Problem

<2-4 paragraphs describing the concrete motivating case, who feels it, and what breaks today. Include evidence when available.>

## Appetite

<One line. "Small batch - 2 weeks." or "Big batch - 6 weeks.">

## Solution

<The shaped concept at fat-marker fidelity. Name the parts and how they connect without turning this into a spec.>

## Rabbit Holes

- <Named unknown> - why it could blow the appetite, and how it is addressed now

## No-gos

- Not <specific out-of-scope item>

## Bonus Features

- <Clearly optional add-on if time remains>

## KPIs

- <How success will be measured>

## Long-Term Vision

<Why this bet matters beyond the immediate cycle.>

## Collaborators

- <Users, teams, or source material that shaped the pitch>
```

## Section Notes

- `Problem`: generic pain statements are not enough; use a real motivating case
- `Appetite`: fixed constraint, never a range
- `Solution`: rough shape, not implementation detail
- `Rabbit Holes`: name the risk and the mitigation
- `No-gos`: state explicit boundaries that will still be useful mid-cycle
